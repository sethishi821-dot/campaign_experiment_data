# Business Problem Statement

The company is running marketing campaigns and needs to determine whether the campaign strategy is generating meaningful business results.

### Decision to be Made

Management must decide whether the campaign should be continued, expanded, modified, or discontinued based on its performance and impact on key business outcomes.

### Who the Decision Impacts

The decision impacts:

- Marketing Team
- Sales Team
- Business Management
- Customers targeted by the campaign
- Company stakeholders responsible for revenue growth

### Metric to Improve

The primary metrics that should improve include:

- Conversion Rate
- Sales Revenue
- Customer Acquisition Rate
- Return on Marketing Investment (ROMI)
- Profitability

Secondary metrics may include:

- Customer Engagement
- Order Volume
- Average Order Value

### Risks to Monitor

The following risks must be monitored:

- Low conversion despite increased campaign spending
- Negative impact on profitability
- High customer acquisition costs
- Poor campaign performance in specific customer segments
- Data quality issues affecting analysis accuracy
- Statistical results driven by random variation rather than true campaign impact

### Evidence Required Before Making a Recommendation

Before making a recommendation, the following evidence is required:

- Comparison of campaign and control group performance
- Conversion rate analysis
- Revenue and profit comparison
- Statistical significance testing
- Customer segment performance analysis
- Data quality validation results
- Risk assessment findings

A recommendation should only be made if sufficient evidence demonstrates that the campaign delivers measurable business value while maintaining acceptable levels of risk.
